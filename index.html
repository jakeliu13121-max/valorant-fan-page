About the Game

Valorant is a 5v5 character-based tactical shooter. Each round, one team attacks and tries to plant a bomb (the "Spike") while the other team defends. Every character (Agent) has unique abilities on top of gunplay, so strategy matters as much as aim.

All Agents

Astra

Controller

A Ghanaian guardian who protects the world from a pocket astral dimension, watching over the battlefield and bending cosmic energy to her will.

  • Gravity Well (Basic) — places a star that pulls in and eventually stuns nearby enemies.
  • Nova Pulse (Basic) — places a star that detonates into a concussive blast.
  • Astral Form / Nebula (Signature) — enters a spectral state to place stars anywhere on the map, which convert into Gravity Well, Nova Pulse, or a smoke.
  • Cosmic Divide (Ultimate) — creates a giant wall between two points that blocks bullets and heavily muffles sound.

Breach

Initiator

A blind, bionic Swedish soldier who fires kinetic and seismic charges through walls to clear a path for his team.

  • Aftershock (Basic) — fires a bolt through a wall that erupts into a damaging fireball on the other side.
  • Fault Line (Basic) — sends a linear seismic quake that dazes everyone it passes through.
  • Flashpoint (Signature) — detonates a blinding charge through a wall.
  • Rolling Thunder (Ultimate) — unleashes a line of rolling quakes that knock up and daze enemies caught in it.

Brimstone

Controller

A steady American commander who calls in orbital strikes and smoke cover to keep his squad in control.

  • Incendiary (Basic) — launches a grenade that creates a lingering damaging fire zone.
  • Stim Beacon (Basic) — deploys a beacon giving nearby teammates a fire-rate/reload boost.
  • Sky Smoke (Signature) — calls in up to three smoke clouds anywhere on the map from a tactical overview.
  • Orbital Strike (Ultimate) — calls down a devastating laser strike on a marked area.

Chamber

Sentinel

A stylish French weapons designer who builds his own custom arsenal to hold down key positions with precision.

  • Trademark (Basic) — places a hidden trap that slows and reveals enemies who trigger it.
  • Rendezvous (Basic) — places two teleport anchors to instantly reposition between them.
  • Headhunter (Signature) — equips a powerful custom heavy pistol for precise picks.
  • Tour De Force (Ultimate) — equips a sniper rifle that one-shots on any hit and slows enemies it damages.

Clove

Controller

An immortal, mischievous Scottish troublemaker who torments enemies in combat and refuses to stay down even in death.

  • Pick-Me-Up (Basic) — after a kill/assist, gain movement speed and temporary overhealing.
  • Meddle (Basic) — throws an orb of essence that decays the health of anyone it hits.
  • Ruse (Signature) — drops one or two smokes, usable even after Clove has died that round.
  • Not Dead Yet (Ultimate) — survives lethal damage for a short time, fully reviving if a kill/assist is earned.

Cypher

Sentinel

A secretive Moroccan information broker who turns the map into his own surveillance network.

  • Trapwire (Basic) — sets an invisible tripwire that reveals and restrains enemies who cross it.
  • Cyber Cage (Basic) — tosses a cage that triggers into a vision-blocking, slowing zone.
  • Spycam (Signature) — places a remote camera to scout enemies and tag them with a dart.
  • Neural Theft (Ultimate) — extracts intel from an eliminated enemy's body to reveal their whole team's location.

Deadlock

Sentinel

A Norwegian operative with experimental nanowire tech who restrains and outlasts enemies rather than out-gunning them.

  • GravNet (Basic) — throws a grenade that forces caught enemies to crouch-walk slowly.
  • Barrier Mesh (Basic) — deploys a disc that grows into a wall of blocking barriers.
  • Sonic Sensor (Signature) — places a sensor that detects noise and concusses enemies nearby.
  • Annihilation (Ultimate) — fires a nanowire that cocoons and drags the first enemy hit unless freed by teammates.

Fade

Initiator

A Turkish nightmare hunter who channels her own fears into monstrous creatures that hunt down and expose enemies.

  • Prowler (Basic) — releases creatures that track toward an enemy's last known position and blind them.
  • Seize (Basic) — throws an orb that anchors down and hinders/suppresses everyone in its area.
  • Haunt (Signature) — launches an eye that reveals enemies in its area to Fade's team.
  • Nightfall (Ultimate) — sends a wave of dark energy that deafens, decays, and reveals enemies it passes.

Gekko

Initiator

A laid-back agent from LA who sends his crew of scrappy creatures ahead to scout, blind, and detain enemies before regrouping to reuse them.

  • Mosh Pit (Basic) — throws a creature that lands then explodes with damage.
  • Wingman (Basic) — sends a creature forward to concuss enemies or plant/defuse the spike.
  • Dizzy (Signature) — sends a floating creature ahead that blinds enemies it gets a clear look at.
  • Thrash (Ultimate) — sends a creature to pounce on and detain an enemy, disabling their weapon and abilities.

An Indian scientist who has mastered ancient elemental water technology, shielding his team behind walls of water.

Duelist

A calm, composed Chinese "fixer" who bends probability itself to shield and duel down enemies one by one.

  • Contingency (Basic) — fires an indestructible wall of energy that blocks bullets and vision.
  • Undercut (Basic) — throws a bolt that passes through walls and briefly weakens anyone it hits.
  • Double Tap (Signature) — channels to gain a damage-absorbing shield and enters a flow state where kills refresh it.
  • Kill Contract (Ultimate) — pulls Iso and the first enemy hit into an isolated arena for a duel to the death.

A quick, confident South Korean daredevil who rides the wind to dash and dive headfirst into fights.

  • Blade Storm (Ultimate) — equips throwing knives that one-shot on a body hit and refresh on kills.

A synthetic soldier built as a weapon against Radiant powers, sent to suppress and destroy enemy agents' abilities.

  • ZERO/point (Signature) — throws a blade that suppresses (disables) the abilities of enemies it hits.
  • NULL/cmd (Ultimate) — overloads with energy, suppressing nearby enemies' abilities and reviving KAY/O with partial health if downed while active.

All Maps

Ascent

An Italian piazza and vineyard town with a fountain-lined plaza at mid; two sites, with remotely closable doors that can briefly seal off key chokepoints.

Split

A vertical, neon-lit Tokyo setting with tight corridors; two sites connected by ziplines and ropes running through a narrow mid, rewarding strong verticality plays.

Fracture

A shattered Radianite research site in Colorado, USA, laid out in an H-shape where attackers can spawn from both ends, surrounding the defenders in the middle.

Bind

A Moroccan desert marketplace with two sites and no direct mid; instead, two one-way teleporters let players cross the map quickly.

Breeze

A sunny Caribbean island map with wide open areas and long sightlines across two sites, favoring long-range duels and wide-swinging duelists.

Abyss

A hidden Arctic fortress atop Jan Mayen island with two sites split by a huge open mid; unique for having no outer boundary, so players can fall to their death off the edges.

Lotus

An ancient underground temple in India with three sites, rotating spinning doors, and destructible walls that open new lines of attack.

Sunset

A compact, sun-baked Los Angeles neighborhood map with two sites close together and a central "Gallery" area, encouraging fast, aggressive rounds.

Pearl

A submerged, futuristic Portuguese city under the ocean (Omega Earth) with two sites connected by canals and mid pathways.

Icebox

A Russian Arctic research site with two sites full of crates, ziplines, and vertical cover, making for chaotic close-quarters fights.

Corrode

A rusted French coastal town (Mont-Saint-Michel) turned Radianite salt-mining facility, blending castle ruins with industrial catwalks; two sites with flooded, noisy walkways in places.

Haven

A hidden Bhutanese monastery map unique for having three bomb sites (A, B, C) instead of the usual two, spreading defenders thin.

Pro Tips (2026)

  • Use a small, static crosshair with no movement/firing error — the competitive standard for precision.
  • Pre-aim at head level where enemies are likely to peek — this habit separates ranks more than raw aim.
  • Keep sound effects volume up so footsteps and gunfire stay clearly audible.
  • Turn V-Sync off to avoid input lag; aim for 144Hz+ if your setup allows it.
  • Pick a sensitivity (eDPI) and stick with it for at least two weeks to build muscle memory.
